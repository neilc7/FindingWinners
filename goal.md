# Goal

Find companies that are **underappreciated right now** and likely to benefit significantly from the AI narrative — before the stock has already made its big move.

## Core Thesis

The market systematically underprices the impact of AI adoption on certain companies. Catalysts like earnings beats, conference announcements, and partnership news create inflection points, but the market often takes weeks or months to fully re-rate. The goal is to identify these catalysts early and build conviction before the crowd catches on.

## What We're Looking For

Signals that a company is entering an AI-driven re-rating:

- **Earnings reports** — Revenue acceleration, margin expansion, or guidance raises tied to AI demand
- **Tech conference announcements** — New AI products, platform launches, or strategic pivots revealed at events
- **Partnership news** — Deals with hyperscalers, AI labs, or enterprise customers that validate the AI story
- **Customer wins / deployment announcements** — Proof that AI revenue is real, not just narrative
- **Capacity expansion** — New data centers, GPU procurement, or infrastructure buildout signaling demand confidence
- **Analyst upgrades / estimate revisions** — Early Wall Street recognition that numbers need to go higher

## The Pattern We Want to Catch

A company reports a strong catalyst, but the stock either:

1. **Reacts modestly** — Good news, muted move. The market hasn't fully digested the implications.
2. **Pulls back after an initial pop** — Earnings spike fades, creating a better entry before the next leg.
3. **Is ignored entirely** — The catalyst happens but the company isn't on anyone's radar yet.

The payoff comes in subsequent quarters as the trend compounds and the market catches up.

## Case Studies

### DELL — Feb 26, 2026 Earnings

- **Catalyst:** Feb 26 earnings report showed strong AI server demand
- **Initial reaction:** Stock jumped $120 -> $140 (~17% move)
- **What happened next:** Subsequent earnings confirmed the trajectory. Stock ran to $310-$420
- **Lesson:** The Feb 26 earnings were the signal. The initial 17% move was just the beginning — the real move was 3-4x from there. Anyone who identified the AI server demand inflection after the first earnings had months to build a position.

### ALAB — May 05, 2026 Earnings

- **Catalyst:** Very strong earnings report
- **Initial reaction:** Stock stayed muted, hovering around $200
- **What happened next:** By May 29, stock reached $342 (~70% move in 24 days)
- **Lesson:** Classic "good earnings, muted reaction" setup. The market needed time to digest and re-rate. The muted initial reaction was the opportunity.

### DOCN — AI Software Orchestration Pivot

- **Catalyst:** Building AI software orchestration platform. Strong earnings trajectory since Aug 2025.
- **Key event:** May 05, 2026 earnings resulted in ~40% stock price increase
- **Current:** At $155 on May 29, 2026
- **Lesson:** The AI pivot was visible for multiple quarters before the big re-rating. Conference commentary and product announcements from mid-2025 onward were early signals.

## What This System Should Do

1. **Scan for catalysts** — Systematically monitor earnings, conferences, partnerships, and news for companies on the watchlist (and discover new ones)
2. **Detect mismatch** — Flag when a strong catalyst produces a muted stock reaction (or no reaction)
3. **Score conviction** — Rank opportunities by information change, business quality, valuation support, catalyst strength, and sentiment opportunity
4. **Track follow-through** — After identifying a catalyst, monitor whether subsequent data confirms the thesis
5. **Surface early** — The whole point is to be early. If everyone already knows the story, we're too late.

## What This System Should NOT Do

- Chase momentum after a stock has already made a big move
- Focus on short-term trading or technical patterns
- Recommend entries based on price action alone
- Ignore bear cases or negative variants

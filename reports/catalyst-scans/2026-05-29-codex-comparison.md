## Potential Discrepancies Or Overstatements

### 1. IREN: NVIDIA warrant/investment should not be treated as a price floor

The existing scan says NVIDIA's option to invest up to $2.1B at $70/share provides a near-term floor. I think that is too strong. IREN's own release frames the warrants as tied to deployment milestones and capacity access, which is validation but not a simple downside floor. If deployment slips, the signaling value weakens.

Verdict: **Agree on bullish mismatch; disagree with “floor” framing.**

### 2. CRM: Agentforce details are directionally right, but the existing scan may overstate precision

The existing scan says Agentforce ARR is $1.2B and AI/Data ARR is $3.4B. Salesforce's official Q1 FY27 release confirms Data + AI ARR above $1B and 120% YoY growth. Some finer-grained figures may come from earnings-call materials or third-party coverage, but I would cite the official release more conservatively unless the transcript/source is attached.

Verdict: **Agree on thesis; use official numbers carefully.**

### 3. RBRK: product launch is real; traction is not yet proven

Rubrik Agent Cloud is a real new product/category move. The existing scan treats it as a meaningful underappreciated catalyst. I agree, but I would not score the information change as highly until Rubrik discloses customer adoption, ARR contribution, or pipeline traction.

Verdict: **Agree with watchlist priority; evidence is still early.**

### 4. AVAV: Iran-conflict demand language needs support

The existing scan cites “Iran conflict driving unprecedented demand.” I did not verify that specific claim from company sources in this pass. The verified evidence I found supports BlueHalo integration, Switchblade orders, and broader defense/autonomy demand, but not that exact catalyst framing.

Verdict: **Treat as unverified unless a source is added.**

### 5. BRUN: evidence quality is thin

The existing scan includes detailed BRUN metrics such as contracted revenue, annual sales, and delayed 10-Q timing. Some public listing/investor-relations evidence exists, but this is still a new public company with limited operating history. I would keep it speculative and wait for filings.

Verdict: **Agree speculative; avoid upgrading before filings.**

### 6. NOW: ServiceNow AI-control-plane thesis is plausible but valuation matters

The existing scan is directionally convincing on Autonomous Workforce and AI Control Tower. I agree on the product narrative, but I would require customer adoption metrics because ServiceNow already trades like a high-quality platform company.

Verdict: **Agree with thesis; valuation reduces mismatch.**

## My Final Read

The prior model did a strong thematic job. I would not throw it out. But I would tighten the process in future scans:

- Separate **verified fact**, **management claim**, **third-party report**, and **analyst inference**.
- Avoid treating customer contracts as revenue until deployment and capacity milestones are visible.
- Penalize already-re-rated names harder, because this framework is explicitly about finding catalysts before the stock moves.
- Add a source table for every ticker so later comparisons are easier.

## Actionable Changes To The Framework

- Add an `evidence_quality` field to each company scan: `official filing`, `company press release`, `transcript`, `analyst note`, `media report`, or `unverified`.
- Add a `price_has_moved` field to distinguish great businesses from early opportunities.
- For contract-heavy AI infrastructure names, track `contracted revenue`, `deployed capacity`, `recognized revenue`, and `financing needs` separately.
